Nine-channeled Pearl

2022

piano quintet

10'
Score
In ancient Chinese tradition Nine-Channeled Pearl is a small wooden ball that has a hole with curved joints. Threading it with a silk thread requires patience, dexterity, calmness, focus, and intention – the use of technique and not force.

Piano quintet Nine-Channeled Pearl (2022) seeks a state of effortless flow. Beginning with rhythmical gamelan-inspired movement it continues through twirling scales that lead to the heart of the piece – a sonorous bell-like harmonic on the piano. This is the moment of stillness and concentration where the silk thread appears on the opposite side of the pearl. From this calm center the music continues floating in a dream-like world ending with a chorale played by the strings.

Circulating your inner energy is just like guiding a thread through the nine-channeled pearl. Then nothing can block the circulation.
- Tai Chi master Wu YuXiang (1812-1880)

Glow within

2018

for flute and bass clarinet

7'
The very low and soft bass clarinet pitches seem to gain a glow, an aura from the high overtones that emerge from them. Together with the chords played by the flute, they are at the same time intense and fragile – they seem to glow from within. I wanted to build a chorale out of these elements, a moment where the music flows slowly and peacefully. This is the heart of the piece. A moment for the thought to rest at ease. All around the music builds on fuzzy textures that are full of movement yet not going anywhere. Fragmented breath with a slight vocal quality moves towards longer lines as if wanting to say something, but no words come out.

Commissioned by Earplay

Lightness of the Days

2017

for bass flute and kantele

11'
Video
From the first moment I heard it I was fascinated by the sound of the multiphonic harmonics by kantele. This bell-like sound brings to mind, surprisingly not the Finnish landscape, but the Californian one. Together with the airy vocal-like sounds of the bass flute they bring to mind the light that shines from a very different sky than the Finnish one. A sky that seems to be further away, with more bright space underneath, and with clouds that stretch with long smooth curves across and towards the sea. I often walk the same routes through the UC Berkeley Campus to get to my workspace. Climbing up the Berkeley hills and towards a serene scenery over the bay. I stay for a while there on top of the stairs to absorb the view. These moments I feel are filled with light and lightness. Through its opposite, the heavy sleepy moments, the lightness gains its character. Commissioned by Eija Kankaanranta and Camilla Hoitenga

soaring

2011

for flute/alto flute and accordion

10'
Video
When I was gathering material for soaring, my first idea was a gust of wind: how it blows in different speeds and rattles the leaves and picks up grains of sand while it blows. A mountain rigde or cliff can deflect the wind upwards. If the wind is strong enough it can provide enough force for birds and even small airplanes to keep them floating in air with no extra force needed.

The capricious wind in the first part of the piece creates a lift on which the second part is soaring (to soar – to glide). The use of energy is slowly reducing towards the end.

Commissioned by and dedicated to Andrea Carola Kiefer.
