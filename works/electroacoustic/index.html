2020

in the universe everyth ng is a circle draws inspiration from the novel by Sawako Nakayasu, Settle Her. Nakayasu's novel was written on a bus during one day, Thanksgiving, when she wanted to protest the meaningless festivities by sitting on a local bus circling the same route the whole day. Some fragments of Nakayasu's text are woven into the contrabass clarinet textures. Occasionally, the vocal qualities of the instrument as well as the electronics push towards the foreground. The piece is a journey in circles towards the stillness of the center.

“I th nk about all the occasions when people refuse or decline knowledge I c n count the prom nent inc dences of the on m two hands and usually there is som th ng I c n say about those moments when people say don’t tell m I don’t want to know.“

Sawako Nakayasu

This piece was created in close collaboration with clarinetist Heather Roche. Commissioned by and dedicated to Roche with the aid of a commissioning grant by the Society of Finnish Composers Sibelius Fund.

Ina Donna

2019

for four female voices (SSSA), electronics, lights and video

14'
Audio
Video
Poetry by Ina Coolbrith (1842–1928) stroke me as being simultaneously filled with lively passion and sorrow. During her lifetime, she was a remarkable figure in the poetic circles of San Francisco, but nowadays her work is completely forgotten. It is not an uncommon story for a female artist balancing between getting financially by and focusing on her art – an equation that is hard to solve even in the present day.

Instead of using Coolbrith's texts as whole, I decided to create a portrait of her – Ina Donna, where Ina as her self-chosen artistic name reflects the passionate commitment to work. Donna, in turn, has a different perspective where she feels left outside and describes her art in a gloomy setting: “This is my heritage, mine own, That alien hands from me withhold. From barred windows, dark and cold, I view, with heart that maketh moan.”

In her art, Ina Donna tries to build the unreachable. She wants her work to live on after she dies. For this portrait, I aimed at building something unreachable and utterly feminine – the moon. I set out to print a 9-inch moon on a 3D printer to see if it would actually work. Through trial and error, several months of work, and after four days of printing the moon was ready. The birds, the “fall of blossom snow upon the ground” of Coolbrith's poetry made me think of Hildegard von Bingen's music and especially her works on the Virgin Mary. When my music embraces Bingen in the 3rd movement, I think the question is left open – whether Ina Donna reaches the moon in her work or not.

Freedom from Fear

2017

for oboe, electronics and lights

12'
Audio
Video
The piece reflects the thoughts of Aung San Suu Kyi, a Burmese politician and freedom fighter. Suu Kyi gained international acclaim and received many honours, including the Nobel Peace Prize, during and after the 15 years she spent in house arrest under the Burmese military dictatorship. I was fascinated with her thought "in any country, if the majority is determined to follow a certain path, they will be able to do it, sooner or later" - which indeed came true when the Burmese dictatorship turned into democracy in the 2010's after the majority of the Burmese people had joined forces. In this piece, "Freedom of Fear" among other famous quotes of Suu Kyi is transcribed for oboe, making the oboe textures represent the strength and power of thought over brutal violence.

...sicut aurora procedit for solo violin (2014) proceeded from the twilight of the dawn to the sun rising above the horizon. The version for violin and electronics (2015) travels this journey backwards, as if in a memory. O frondens virga, an antiphone by Hildegard von Bingen, is intertwined with the violin part in the beginning of the work. This antiphone, placed in a distant chatedral-like space is a reflection of the brightness of the day. The words of the antiphone are bathing in bright hope. The first rays of light of the rising sun hit the outer layers of the atmosphere thus making them glow. Through making the sound particles spin, the movement glow radiates in translucent harmonies. The memory of the twilight of the dawn ends the work in the atmosphere of the blue hour. The dawn is rising, but the sun is still waiting beneath the horizon.

The piece can be performed with choreography for an aerialist/dancer.

Aina on tänään - It is always today

2015

radioplay for 2-24 channels

28'
Audio
Video
This dreamlike radiophonic piece is an inner journey from conflict and isolation towards resolution and opening up again. What has happened remains for the listener to interpret. The piece begins with the narrator wrapping inside their thoughts, being on an island and being an island itself. Flashes from another world, a busy airport where all emotions die and are 'torn to pieces in the agonising cold', pass through. The aphoristic poems by Vilja-Tuulia Huotarinen are contrasted with the ones by Elke de Rijcke. It is always today rotates around the thoughts of the narrator, and all days seem to be alike. The title reflects to the piece also in another way: every single moment there's a chance, a possibility to start over and build into a different ending.

The duration of the concert version is 10 minutes.

Earthship

2015

for ensemble and fixed media

10'
Audio
Earthship is a house that is designed to be in harmony with the surrounding nature. The building materials are natural and recycled – used tires, soda cans, bottles, clay, stones. The energy efficient building is designed to run mostly on solar power. The building collects its own water, and food is grown in its greenhouse. Rather than resisting the forces of nature, this building is nurtured by them. Like the building, this piece builds on natural and recycled material. It travels through rich natural habitats and draws portraits of its residents. The sounds of endangered species of insects and animals, threatened by extinction due to the destruction of nature, are included in the electronic part. Earthship was composed for the final round of Feeding Music International Composition Contest 2015 hosted by Divertimento Ensemble.

Après tel février

2013

for female voice and electronics

5'
Audio
Après tel février is in a way a study of the human voice and its manipulation with the means of electronics. The electronic part multiplies the singer into a small group of singers which are then set into dialogue with each other. Mezzo-soprano Anaïs Bertrand and I found a natural way for her to produce a broken, multiphonique tone. The poems of Elke de Rijcke, with the physical sensation they give, possessed the right kind of an atmosphere and structure for this powerful, ripping yet beautiful timbre. The poem Après tel février – Piliers de béton paints the portrait of a woman during the last months of pregnancy, when the badly slept nights start to govern the days. She doesn't go out, but paces restlessly small circles inside her flat. The solemn but strong atmosphere of the poem is reflected in the fragile breaths and strong expressiveness in the music.
